# Food Match

Ever tried to pick a restaurant with someone and realized you have no idea what they actually like? You end up at the same safe choices, or someone compromises and doesn't enjoy it.

Meanwhile, a research project called Epicure just scanned 4 million recipes across 7 languages and mapped how 1,790 ingredients actually relate to each other in the real world. Soy sauce and vanilla share over 100 flavor compounds. Ghee and cumin live in the same recipe neighborhoods. The ingredient universe has a geometry, and Epicure found it.

Food Match turns that research into a 5-minute taste quiz. You rate 30 ingredients, the app maps you onto cuisine directions and flavor neighborhoods, then tells you what you'd probably love, what to try next, and — when you share your profile link with a friend — what you'd both enjoy eating together.

It's a static web app. No accounts, no backend, no data collection. Your profile lives in a URL you can text someone. They take the quiz, and the app instantly shows what you have in common, where you differ, and what to eat together tonight.
